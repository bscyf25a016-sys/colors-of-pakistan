Overview

Paradise on Earth

Gilgit-Baltistan is the meeting point of the world's three highest mountain ranges: the Himalayas, the Karakoram, and the Hindu Kush. It is a mountaineer's paradise.

Historically part of the Silk Road connecting China to the subcontinent, it is home to diverse cultures and languages like Shina, Balti, and Burushaski speakers.

History & Geography

Ancient Silk Road

The region was a vital passage for trade and Buddhism between China and South Asia. Rock carvings throughout the area depict its rich history. It gained independence in 1947 and voluntarily joined Pakistan.

Glaciers & Peaks

Home to 5 peaks above 8,000m, including K2 (8,611m) and Nanga Parbat. It also boasts the longest glaciers outside the polar regions: Biafo, Baltoro, and Batura.

Economy

The economic lifeline connecting Pakistan to China.

Agriculture

Tourism

A major hub for international mountaineering and trekking expeditions.

Culture & Lifestyle

Attire

The Pakol (woolen cap) with a feather is a symbol of identity.

People

The people are known for their hospitality and longevity, especially in Hunza.

Cuisine

Dishes include Mamtu (dumplings) and Salty Butter Tea.

Polo

The "Game of Kings" is played here in its wildest form at the Shandur Festival.
