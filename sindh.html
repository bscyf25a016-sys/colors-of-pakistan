Overview

Land of Sufis

Sindh is the second-largest province of Pakistan by population and serves as the country's economic hub. It is historically known as the "Mehran" and has been the seat of the Indus Valley Civilization for thousands of years.

Home to the bustling metropolis of Karachi, Sindh is a land of contrasts—from the fertile plains of the Indus to the arid Thar Desert and the pristine coastline of the Arabian Sea. It is deeply rooted in Sufi mysticism, tolerance, and rich folklore.

Heritage & Land

History & Geography

Gateway of Islam

Sindh's history is ancient, highlighted by the ruins of Mohenjo-daro (2500 BCE). It was the first region in the subcontinent to embrace Islam when Muhammad bin Qasim conquered it in 712 CE.

Throughout history, it has been ruled by the Soomra, Samma, Kalhora, and Talpur dynasties, each leaving a rich architectural legacy like the Makli Necropolis.

From Desert to Sea

Sindh features a diverse landscape. To the east lies the vast Thar Desert, bordering India. The west is flanked by the Kirthar Mountains.

The center is irrigated by the Indus River, while the south boasts a coastline along the Arabian Sea, home to the bustling ports of Karachi and Bin Qasim.

Growth Engine

Economy

Hub

Economic Powerhouse

Karachi contributes a massive share to Pakistan's GDP, housing the stock exchange, head offices of major corporations, and two sea ports.

Industry & Manufacturing

Sindh has a strong industrial base, producing textiles, cement, steel, and automobiles in zones like SITE and Korangi.

Maritime Trade

The Port of Karachi and Port Qasim handle the vast majority of Pakistan's international trade cargo.

Energy Resources

Rich in natural resources, including coal reserves in Thar and significant natural gas fields.

Vibrant Life

Culture & Lifestyle

The Ajrak & Topi

The Ajrak (block-printed shawl) and Sindhi Topi are iconic symbols of Sindhi culture and pride, worn with Shalwar Kameez.

Spicy Cuisine

Famous for Sindhi Biryani, Palla Fish, and Sai Bhaji. The food is known for being spicy and flavorful.

Sufism

The land of saints like Shah Abdul Latif Bhittai and Lal Shahbaz Qalandar. Their shrines are centers of spiritual gathering and music.

Malakhro

The traditional wrestling sport of Sindh, played at festivals. Cricket is also massively popular, with Karachi being a cricket hub.
